Basic and Additional
Childcare Subsidies

Government subsidies for childcare and infant care are available for parents of children who are Singapore citizens enrolled at licensed preschools in Singapore. It is important to note that these subsidies are applicable only to Singapore Citizens; Permanent Residents and foreigners are required to pay the standard gross fees.

The basic subsidy for childcare programmes is set at $300, while the subsidy for infant care is at $600. For families with working mothers and a gross monthly household income of $12,000 or below, an Additional Subsidy is available, further reducing out-of-pocket costs. To ensure equitable support, larger families with 3 or more dependents can also apply to have their additional subsidy computed on a Per Capita Income (PCI) basis, potentially qualifying them for higher subsidy tiers based on their specific household needs.

For instance, for a working mother (as the main applicant) with a household of 4 members and a total household income of $6,000. The monthly price for a Full Day Childcare programme at our POP childcare centre in Ang Mo Kio would look like this:

Gross Fee: $650
Basic Subsidy: $300
Additional Subsidy: Based on PCI Tier, estimated up to $340* 
Final Out-of-Pocket: $10 per month

*Subject to changes by ECDA

Frequently Asked Questions About
Infant Care and Childcare Fees in Singapore

How much does infant care cost in Singapore after subsidies at POP centres?

At a POP centre like Little Footprints Preschool, full-day infant care fees are capped at $1,290 (before GST). For Singaporean families with working mothers, a basic subsidy of $600 brings the monthly cost down to approximately $690, with further reductions possible through additional subsidies.

What childcare subsidies can Singaporean families get at Little Footprints Preschool?

Singaporean families are entitled to a Basic Subsidy of $300 for childcare and $600 for infant care. Working mothers with a gross monthly household income of $12,000 or below may also qualify for an Additional Subsidy, significantly lowering the total out-of-pocket expenses for high-quality education.

Are the prices different between POP and non-POP preschools in Singapore?

Yes, POP centres adhere to government-mandated fee caps, currently $650 for childcare and $1,290 for infant care (before GST), to ensure affordability. In contrast, non-POP or private preschools have the flexibility to set their own rates based on premium facilities or specialised enrichment, which can range from $1,650 - $3,515 per month.

How can I estimate my out-of-pocket childcare or infant care fees ?

To get a clear estimate, subtract the Basic Subsidy (and any applicable Additional Subsidy) from the centre's gross fee; for example, a $650 childcare fee at a POP centre becomes $460 after the $300 basic subsidy.
